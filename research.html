CFD case study 03 · Rotating machinery

Vertical axis wind turbine — rotating frame analysis

Modeled airflow over a VAWT rotor using a rotating reference frame to characterize lift and energy extraction across a full revolution.

VAWT k-ε Realizable Rotating frame Turbulence Renewable energy

Setup

  • Solver: k-ε Realizable
  • Mesh: 42,491 cells
  • Boundary conditions: 10 m/s inlet velocity · 40 rpm rotation
  • Assumptions: steady, viscous, no-slip, incompressible

Results

  • Velocity deficit and turbulent kinetic energy in the rotor wake confirmed momentum transfer from flow to rotor
  • Blade orientation relative to incoming flow had a strong effect on instantaneous torque production
  • Favorable blade angles generated significantly higher lift, consistent with the Betz limit framework
CFD case study 01 · Airfoil aerodynamics

Symmetric airfoil — parametric AoA sweep

Analyzed a NACA 0012 airfoil at Mach 0.7 across a 0°–5° angle of attack sweep, validated against published experimental data.

NACA 0012 SST k-ω Mach 0.7 Parametric sweep Validation

Setup

  • Solver: SST k-ω
  • Mesh: 36,800 cells
  • Boundary conditions: Mach 0.7 inlet · 1,000 Pa inlet pressure
  • Assumptions: steady, inviscid, incompressible
  • AoA sweep: 0°, 1°, 2°, 3°, 4°, 5°

Results

  • Pressure and Mach distributions confirmed expected lift generation and boundary layer propagation
  • Simulated lift coefficients fell within acceptable tolerance of published experimental values
  • Validated the Fluent setup for use on the less well-characterized geometries that followed
CFD case study 02 · Internal flow

Axisymmetric nozzle — compressible flow validation

Modeled compressible flow through a converging-diverging nozzle (A = x² + 0.1) and compared results against analytical isentropic nozzle theory.

Nozzle flow Compressible Isentropic theory Validation Inviscid

Setup

  • Geometry: Axisymmetric nozzle, A = x² + 0.1
  • Mesh: 533 cells
  • Boundary conditions: 101,325 Pa reservoir pressure · 300 K reservoir temperature
  • Assumptions: unsteady, inviscid, compressible

Results

  • Simulated and analytical pressures initially appeared to diverge
  • Traced to standard nozzle theory's assumption of uniform flow across each cross-section
  • Wall and centerline pressure sampling showed the analytical value fell exactly between the two, confirming simulation accuracy
