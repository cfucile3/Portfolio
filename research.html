CFD Case Study 01 · Airfoil Aerodynamics

Symmetric Airfoil — Parametric AoA Sweep

Analysis of the aerodynamic behavior of a NACA 0012 airfoil at Mach 0.7, sweeping angles of attack from 0° through 5° to quantify the relationship between AoA and flow behavior. Validated against published experimental data.

Methodology

  • Geometry: NACA 0012
  • Solver: SST k-ω
  • Mesh: 36,800 cells
  • Boundary conditions: Inlet Mach 0.7 · Inlet pressure 1,000 Pa
  • Assumptions: steady, inviscid, incompressible
  • AoA sweep: 0°, 1°, 2°, 3°, 4°, 5°
Results Pressure and Mach distributions confirm expected lift generation and boundary layer propagation. Simulated lift coefficients fell within acceptable tolerance of published experimental values, validating the CFD setup for subsequent studies. This case established confidence in using Fluent to model aerodynamic behavior before moving to less well-characterized geometries.

CFD Case Study 02 · Internal Flow

Axisymmetric Nozzle — Compressible Flow Validation

Analysis of compressible flow through an axisymmetric converging-diverging nozzle defined by A = x² + 0.1 over −0.5 < x < 0.5. Simulated results were compared to analytical isentropic nozzle theory to assess solver accuracy.

Methodology

  • Geometry: Axisymmetric nozzle A = x² + 0.1
  • Mesh: 533 cells
  • Boundary conditions: Reservoir pressure 101,325 Pa · Reservoir temperature 300 K
  • Assumptions: unsteady, inviscid, compressible
Results Initial comparison showed deviation between simulated and analytical pressures. This was traced to the assumption in standard nozzle theory that flow properties are uniform across each cross-section. Sampling pressure at both the wall and centerline showed the analytical value fell between the two, confirming the simulation's physical accuracy and highlighting the limits of 1D theory for 2D geometries.

CFD Case Study 03 · Rotating Machinery

Vertical Axis Wind Turbine — Rotating Frame Analysis

Analysis of the aerodynamic behavior of a vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) using a rotating reference frame. Flow over the rotor blades was computed across multiple rotational positions to characterize lift and energy extraction.

Methodology

  • Solver: k-ε Realizable
  • Mesh: 42,491 cells
  • Boundary conditions: Inlet velocity 10 m/s · Rotation 40 rpm
  • Assumptions: steady, viscous, no-slip, incompressible
  • Rotating frame of reference applied to rotor domain
Results Velocity deficit and turbulent kinetic energy in the rotor wake confirmed momentum transfer from the flow to the rotor. Blade orientation relative to the incoming flow had a strong effect on instantaneous torque production — blades at favorable angles generated significantly higher lift, consistent with VAWT behavior and the Betz limit framework.
